Starting a Branch

Instruments for Change runs through local chapters of individual schools. If you are a student and would like to help bring arts to low-income students in your area, you can start a chapter of your own. A chapter needs the following:

Directors. The number can vary based on the size and scope of your projects; we recommend a minimum of two and a maximum of five. Directors oversee communication with venues, finances, and recruitment of volunteers. They may also perform at benefit concerts.

Requirements:
  1. Chapters must host at least 3 concerts per year, which could include retirement home concerts, community concerts, and/or benefit concerts.
  2. Chapters must register with the "Start a Branch" button below
  3. Chapters must submit information about each event with the "Submit Event Info" button below.

Instruments for Change operates to bring music and all associated benefits to low-income youth. Therefore, the money raised from a chapter’s benefit concerts goes towards organizations that further that cause. Right now, we are operating on a basis of having one organization per year as a destination for all funds raised. You will also need the the certainty that student volunteer performers will be available for events. Contact either of the Mercer Island High School Chapter Directors for more information if you would like to create a chapter.

Organizing a Benefit Recital


1) Contact the venue you'd like to perform at. Country clubs work well. Let them give you 3-5 dates that work for them in a general window you provide (let's say, 4:00 p.m. or later weekdays and anytime on the weekends).

2) Make a poll in the IFC group including all the possible time.

3) Choose the time that works for the most people.

4) After you think everyone who's going to see the post has responded, contact everyone who expressed interest and individually confirm that they can come.

5) See which instruments are represented.

6) If you need more people for a diverse, complete program, reach out to other individuals. Try to get a variety of instrumentation.

7) Contact the venue to have them pen the time into their calendar.

8) Regularly and persistently check in with everyone who expressed interest. Try to get them to confirm and mark their calendars.

9) Let the performers arrange their own transportation and rehearsal schedules, but ask if anyone needs help with those.

10) A week before the event, finalize the program.

11) Confirm with the venue three days before to make a final

12) The day of the event or the night before, print the program. Make it look nice!
